The following clauses must be achieved by the software platform made by AI -
 
1.	The software should be capable of reading  database which consist of all diagnostic parameters.
    > **AI Implementation:** Instead of requiring manual configuration for every sensor format, an AI engine provides *Automatic Data Mapping*. The AI autonomously reads the structure of any uploaded raw database, understands its schema, and instantly normalizes it into our standard format, reducing setup times for new datasets to zero.

2.	The software should be capable of reading from Json file which consist of all the photos of defects. 
    > **AI Implementation:** The platform is designed to natively interface with standard Computer Vision outputs (e.g., COCO JSON format). This means WebOne interprets the "bounding boxes" generated by Machine Learning models (like YOLO or Mask R-CNN) from the diagnostic trains' cameras, automatically correlating the geometric defect to the exact photographic evidence.

3.	The software should be capable of visualizing the parameters plot in reference of km in X axis and parameter value in Y axis.
    > **AI Implementation:** The AI semantically analyzes the database content and auto-configures the charts. It independently recognizes the "Kilometric Progression" column for the X-axis and selects the most critical parameters for the Y-axis, delivering a ready-to-use, "smart" graph without manual variable selection by the operator.

4.	The software should be capable of summarizing the over threshold alarms in T1 - T4 four categories. 
    > **AI Implementation:** Beyond rigid standard-based categorization (e.g., EN 13231-3), a *Machine Learning Anomaly Detection* engine filters out false positives caused by sensor noise. It automatically generates an **intelligent text report** (e.g., "Warning: anomalous concentration of T4 alarms in section Km 12-14, visual inspection recommended"), summarizing hours of data into immediately actionable feedback.

5.	The software shall allow quick access to the image of the selected defects.
    > **AI Implementation:** The AI acts as a "semantic bridge". By clicking on a peak in the Cartesian graph, the intelligent search algorithm instantly retrieves the exact video frame or photo corresponding to that millisecond/kilometer, visually highlighting the crack or anomaly recognized by the neural network.

6.	The software shall be capable of performing chainage km section analysis either in auto way or manual way. 
    > **AI Implementation:** In "Auto" mode, the AI uses *Clustering algorithms* to automatically segment the entire railway line into "homogeneous" sections based on wear status or degradation type. The operator no longer has to guess: the software autonomously highlights the kilometer sections that behave anomalously compared to the average.

7.	The software shall be able to define the scales of the possible measurement graphs available via the HMI.
    > **AI Implementation:** Through *Intelligent Auto-Scaling*, the graphs do more than just mechanical zooming. The AI recognizes anomalous values (outliers), isolates them to avoid "flattening" the graph, and dynamically adapts the scale to perfectly highlight the defect to the operator's eyes, guaranteeing the best possible Human-Machine Interface (HMI) experience.

8.	The software shall be able to define the area including its distance chosen on the same line, and compare the plot of multi acquisitions
    > **AI Implementation:** Here the AI provides crucial value through *Automatic Spatial Data Alignment*. When overlapping two measurements taken months apart, sensors often record slight kilometric shifts (e.g., due to wheel slip). The AI recognizes the "signatures" of the defects and elastically aligns the two graphs, ensuring that kilometer 10+500 of the first survey corresponds exactly to kilometer 10+500 of the second.

9.	The software shall be able to select the type of defects that is wishes to display.
    > **AI Implementation:** Instead of making the user navigate through dozens of complex filters, a predictive approach is integrated: the AI recommends the types of defects that are statistically most relevant for that specific line. Furthermore, Natural Language Processing (NLP) allows users to query the system conversationally (e.g., "Show me only the critical corrugation defects").

10.	The software shall be capable of analysis on the trend of the measurement results. 
    > **AI Implementation:** This is the core of **Predictive Maintenance**. The AI studies the history of past surveys, calculates the specific degradation curve for that track section, and predicts when a defect (e.g., currently T1) will worsen to reach a critical alarm (T4). We don't just tell you how the track is today; we forecast its condition in 6 months, optimizing maintenance budgets.
